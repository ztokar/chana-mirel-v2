Cake Smash & First Birthday

Baltimore cake smash photographer.

The cake smash isn't just cake. It's three shoots bundled into one studio session: a clean first-birthday portrait, the cake-on-face moment, and a milk bath at the end. Baby gets tired, cleaned up, and you walk out with three completely different looks.

Inquire about a session

The bundle

Three looks in one visit.

First, a clean first-birthday portrait. Simple outfit, simple backdrop, a few of them you can send to grandparents or print for the card. Then the cake. Baby goes in, usually tentatively, then commits. The mess is the point.

I bundled all of this on purpose. Families kept asking about "first birthday pictures" and "cake smash" like they were different things. They're not. You get both in one session, plus the milk bath at the end. One trip, three looks, and baby's asleep by the time you hit the car.

The milk bath

The part nobody expects.

Most families book the bundle for the cake and end up ordering the milk bath as wall art. Warm milk, a few petals, baby playing quietly. The pictures look painterly. I've had dads call it the favorite picture in the house.

There's a practical reason it comes last too. Baby's had their fill of cake, they're sticky, they're ready to be cleaned up. The milk bath doubles as cleanup. You leave with a dry, tired baby.

Common questions

Frequently asked

What's the best age for a cake smash?
11 to 13 months. Sitting independently and beginning to grab. Too early and baby can't reach the cake; too late and they're overwhelmed by mess.
Do I bring the cake?
Cake is provided. Tell me your color palette and dietary notes when you book.
How messy is it?
Very. That's the point. The studio handles cleanup. Parents bring a towel for the car ride home.
Can siblings or parents be in the portraits?
Yes. The clean first-birthday portion includes family shots. The cake and milk bath are usually baby-only.
When should I book?
Two to four weeks ahead. Holiday slots around December go faster.
