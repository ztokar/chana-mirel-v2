Sitter & Baby Sessions

Baltimore baby photographer.

Sitter sessions photograph the in-between stage. Baby's out of the newborn window, not yet running around at one year, and starting to show real personality. Three to twelve months. Most families come in around six or seven.

Inquire about a session

The stage

Three to twelve months.

The sitter window is where you catch personality. Baby sits up, grabs things, smiles on purpose. They fit in small wraps and themed setups but aren't yet pulling to stand and running off. Most families book around month six or seven.

Before three months, baby can't really sit. After twelve, we're closer to cake smash territory. The six-to-eight-month sweet spot gives you the personality without the chaos.

How it runs

Simple rompers, neutral backdrops.

Same studio as the newborn sessions: warm, private, already set up. I style these simpler than newborn — rompers, wrap-skirts, neutral backgrounds. Parents bring a favorite outfit if they want a specific look.

Sessions take about an hour. Parents and siblings can jump in. A lot of families book this as the middle stop between the newborn session and the first-birthday cake smash.

Common questions

Frequently asked

What age is best for a sitter session?
6 to 8 months. Sitting up, smiling, not yet crawling everywhere.
Is this the same as a milestone session?
Close. Milestone often means the cake smash at one year. Sitter sessions fall in between, around six months.
Do I bring outfits?
Studio wardrobe is provided. Bring favorites if you want specific looks.
How long is the session?
About 60 minutes.
When will I see the gallery?
Two weeks after the session.
