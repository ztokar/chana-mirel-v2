Newborn

Studio vs in-home newborn photography.

Studio sessions give you more consistent results. In-home trades some of that consistency for comfort. I run studio-first, with in-home available for an extra fee in specific situations.

Why the studio wins on consistency

I keep the studio at 80 degrees, which keeps newborns sleepy. Lighting's controlled. Every wrap, every prop, every backdrop is already here. The results actually look like the portfolio you picked me for.

At home, lighting shifts hour to hour. Clutter creeps into the background. The variables pile up.

When in-home actually makes sense

If there's a medical reason you can't travel, in-home is the right call. Same if you've got multiples and moving them is a real production.

For families who strongly prefer home and understand the tradeoffs, in-home is available for an extra fee. Same posing, same wraps, same retouching, just in your house instead of mine.

The compromise most families land on

Studio for the newborn session at five to fourteen days. That's when the posed wraps and buckets matter most.

Lifestyle family photos at home a few months later, once baby's more alert and you're actually back on your feet.

That combo gets you the artistic portraits without asking you to host a photographer during the newborn blur.
