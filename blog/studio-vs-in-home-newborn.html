Newborn

Studio vs in-home newborn photography.

Studio newborn sessions deliver more consistent results. In-home sessions trade that consistency for comfort. At Chana Mirel Photography, sessions are studio-first, with in-home as a fee-based option in limited cases.

Why the studio wins on consistency

The studio is heated to 80 degrees, which keeps newborns calm and sleepy. Lighting is controlled. Every wrap, prop, and backdrop is right there. Results look like the portfolio you picked.

At home, lighting changes hour to hour. Clutter shows up in the background. The variables multiply.

When in-home makes sense

If a medical condition prevents travel, in-home is the right call. Same for multiples that can't easily travel.

For families that strongly prefer home and understand the tradeoffs, it's available for an additional fee. We still use the same posing, same wraps, same retouching, just in your space.

The compromise most families choose

Studio for the newborn (5-14 days). That's the day the posed wraps and buckets matter most.

Lifestyle family photos at home later, once baby is a few months old and you're back on your feet.

This gives you the artistic portraits without forcing a newborn trip when you're exhausted.
