Investment

Newborn photography investment guide.

Newborn photography at a full-service Baltimore studio isn't just a file. You're paying for the session, the experience, and the art. Here's what that actually breaks down into.

The session itself

Two to three hours of private studio time, just you and me. Feeding breaks, outfit changes, sibling shots — all built in. No time pressure.

The studio's heated, stocked, and private. Wraps, bonnets, buckets, backdrops — all already here. You walk in with baby and a feeding bottle; I handle the rest.

The experience

This is what separates a full-service studio from a twenty-minute mall session. Guidance through posing. Patience with long feeds. A trained hand for safety positions.

Eight years and 500-plus Baltimore newborns means the day runs smoothly, baby stays comfortable, and the portraits actually look like the portfolio you booked from — not a watered-down version of it.

The gallery and the art

A professionally retouched gallery lands in your inbox in two to three weeks. Print-ready files. Album design if you want it. Museum-quality wall art ordering built right into the gallery.

Most clients order a mix: digital files, an album, and one or two wall art pieces. The album becomes the first heirloom. The wall art becomes the daily reminder of how small they actually were.

How to get details

I share specific collection pricing over a quick call or email — it's easier to talk through than to paste in a page. Send me a note and I'll walk you through the options.
