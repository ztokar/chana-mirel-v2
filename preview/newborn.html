Newborn Sessions

Sleepy, soft, and exactly how you remember them.

The first two weeks pass in a blur. A studio newborn session with Chana captures the size of their hands, the curl of their feet, the way they fold into themselves before they uncurl into the world.

Book a session
When to come in

Between five and fourteen days old.

Sessions land best in the first two weeks. That is when babies are still sleepy enough for the curled-up poses, the wraps, and the props. After day fourteen they wake up to the world and the session shifts to alert portraits, which are also lovely but a different look.

If you are reading this still pregnant, get on the calendar now and we will pencil in a tentative window. We finalize the date once baby arrives.

The session

Three to four hours, on baby's clock.

Newborns set the pace. We feed when they want to feed and we work between naps. Most sessions run three to four hours, sometimes longer if there is fussing or a diaper change to wait out. The studio is warm and quiet so they settle quickly.

You are welcome to bring a partner, snacks, and anything that helps you relax. Most parents are exhausted and that is fine. The studio is set up so you can rest while we work with baby.

Common questions

How long does a newborn session take?

Three to four hours, sometimes longer. We work around feeding and naps.

Do you provide outfits and props?

Yes. The studio has wraps, hats, headbands, and a full prop wardrobe. Nothing to buy in advance.

What if my baby is fussy?

Normal and expected. We pause, feed, soothe, and try again. The schedule is built for it.

When will I get my photos?

Galleries are delivered within three to four weeks of your session.

Ready?
